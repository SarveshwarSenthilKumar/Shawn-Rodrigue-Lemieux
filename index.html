About Shawn

The inspiring journey of Montreal's chess prodigy, from school tournaments to the world stage

From Montreal to the World

Born in 2004 in Montreal, Shawn Rodrigue-Lemieux learned chess at age 6 and quickly rose through the ranks. By 12, he had a FIDE rating over 2000. In 2022, he won the World Under 18 Chess Championship, becoming only the second Canadian to win a World Youth title and the first Quebecer to do so. Shawn completed his Grandmaster title in 2023 at age 19, officially awarded in 2024, making him the youngest GM in Quebec's history.

Shawn is known for his creative and unorthodox style, often employing rare openings like 1.a3 to surprise his opponents. His perfect 9/9 score at the 2022 Charlotte IM C Norm Invitational (3037 performance) drew international attention, including coverage by GothamChess. He has held top Canadian and world youth rankings and is a regular competitor in elite international events.

Playing Style

Shawn is known for his creative, unorthodox approach, often employing rare openings and dynamic play to outmaneuver his opponents. He is especially famous for using 1.a3 and other offbeat lines, and for his resilience in complex positions.

Notable Traits

  • Prefers creative, non-mainstream openings
  • Strong in both classical and rapid formats
  • Excellent endgame technique and tactical vision
  • Known for psychological preparation and adaptability
